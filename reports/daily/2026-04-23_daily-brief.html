Driving Style Research — Daily Brief

2026-04-23  ·  Research Cycle Summary
Executive Summary
This cycle delivered the first vehicle-class-differentiated deceleration thresholds (8 m/s² for cars, 7 m/s² for trucks) and a Bayesian scoring framework capable of ~7-second near-real-time latency, substantially closing the Safety & Force Thresholds gap and demonstrating production-ready alerting feasibility. Behaviour metrics and scoring remain saturated at 99%, with advanced academic methods — continuous-time hidden Markov models and federated privacy-preserving scoring — confirming the canonical four-metric composite architecture. The dose-response cost model remains the single most critical research gap, blocking ROI calculator and predictive-maintenance module development and keeping the Cost & Maintenance Impact pillar at only 62%.

Top 3 Promising Leads
1 Harsh Braking Incidents — Near-Miss Accidents and HBIs
1library.net
Provides the first peer-sourced vehicle-class-stratified thresholds (8 m/s² cars, 7 m/s² trucks), enabling fleet service configurations that reduce false positives for mixed-fleet operators without waiting for a complete academic lookup table.
2 Driving Behavior Recognition and Scoring: A Bayesian Approach
ScienceDirect
Demonstrates ~7-second near-real-time scoring latency via a Bayesian sequential credibility-band method, directly validating the feasibility of in-cab alerting at sub-10-second intervals for live driver coaching products.
3 Evaluating the Long-Term Effects of Telematics Driver Feedback
ntua.gr
Empirically confirms that feedback effects decay over time, establishing a mandatory design constraint: fleet coaching and engagement modules must deliver continuous adaptive reinforcement rather than one-shot onboarding interventions to sustain safety gains.
Today's Strategic Position

The project is approximately 80% ready to enter product design for core scoring and in-cab alerting modules, with near-saturated evidence on what to measure, a working vehicle-class threshold table, and real-time scoring feasibility confirmed. The dose-response cost model is the single highest-priority research gap: without quantified links between harsh-event frequency and component failure intervals, ROI calculators and predictive-maintenance modules — the primary premium differentiators — cannot be built. Secondary priorities are completing the full LCV/SUV/articulated-truck/bus threshold table with validated sensitivity/specificity, and gathering evidence-based alert-cadence optimisation data to inform suppression logic design.
