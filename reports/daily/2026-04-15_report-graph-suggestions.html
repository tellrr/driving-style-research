Report & Graph Suggestions

Evolving design · updated from daily research · 2026-04-15 · 10 cards

Driver Risk Score Trend
Line ChartConfidence Band
Dimensions: X: week/month · Y: composite risk score (0–100) · Lines per driver · Fleet average overlay · Shaded credibility confidence band (widens for new drivers, narrows with exposure)
Research basis: CTHMM anomaly scoring AUC 0.86; XGBoost credibility blending shows uncertainty declining with exposure; multivariate credibility models provide actuarial framework for dynamic updating
Audience: Fleet manager · Operations director
Speed Compliance Percentile Chart
Area / Ribbon Chart
Dimensions: X: trip distance (km) · Y: speed (km/h) · Shaded ribbons: 25th/50th/75th/95th fleet percentiles · Driver trace overlaid · Speed limit as reference line
Research basis: Percentile-based definitions reduce alert fatigue while maintaining detection of dangerous behaviour; static speed limits frequently misclassify normal driving
Audience: Driver (weekly report) · Fleet manager
Harsh Event Geographic Heat Map
Spatial Heat Map
Dimensions: Map overlay · Colour intensity by harsh event density (braking / acceleration / cornering) · Filterable by driver, vehicle class, time period, event type · Intersection & road geometry context
Research basis: Road geometry (grade, curvature, intersections) independently elevates acceleration events; contextual road attributes are the most critical factors for correct event attribution
Audience: Fleet manager · Operations director (route planning)
Driver Risk Segmentation
Stacked BarSankey Flow
Dimensions: Bars per depot/fleet · Segments by latent risk class (Low / Moderate / High / Very-High) · Sankey panel shows quarter-on-quarter class transitions
Research basis: Latent class analysis shows treating drivers as homogeneous underestimates risk variance and misclassifies high-risk individuals; five risk tiers derived from near-miss surrogates
Audience: Operations director · Fleet manager
True Risk vs. Road Context
Scatter PlotMarginal Distributions
Dimensions: X: road context difficulty index (grade + curvature + speed limit + weather + night) · Y: raw harsh event count · Point size: trip length · Colour: driver · Regression: expected events given context
Research basis: Context-aware models reduce false-positive risk alerts by ~15%; road gradient directly influences risk profiles; fairness in driver comparison requires context adjustment
Audience: Fleet manager (fairness in driver comparison)
Weekly Driver Behaviour Report
NLG TextSparklines
Dimensions: Speeding %, harsh braking count, harsh acceleration count, risk score · Week-over-week trend arrows · Peer percentile rank · 12-week risk sparkline · One specific improvement recommendation
Research basis: NLG feedback reduces speeding behaviour; RCT shows 11–25% reduction from periodic structured feedback; delayed (not immediate) feedback preserves driver effort and engagement
Audience: Driver (delivered via app or email weekly)
Eco-Driving Performance
Scatter Plot
Dimensions: X: aggressive driving composite (harsh accel + harsh brake + jerk magnitude) · Y: fuel consumption (L/100km) · Colour: eco/moderate/aggressive · Shape: vehicle class (car/van/HGV) · Tooltip: driver ID + route
Research basis: Real-world fuel consumption varies significantly by corridor and driver behaviour; jerk analysis links driving style to fuel economy; accelerometer-based detection correlates with fuel consumption
Audience: Fleet manager · Operations director (cost control)
