Executive Summary

This cycle's defining contribution is a convergence of peer-reviewed and practitioner evidence that finally gives the product team actionable building blocks across scoring, thresholds, and dashboard design — but the Cost & Maintenance pillar remains the single commercial bottleneck, now advancing to 55% on the strength of the first XGBoost tire-wear feature-importance study and a Dutch truck damage-prediction model. Critically, the i-DREAMS cross-national naturalistic driving study establishes that intervention modality (real-time vs. post-trip) must be calibrated by driver cultural context, a finding with direct implications for any multi-market fleet product rollout. The core scoring and alerting engine is ready to build; closing dose-response curves for brake wear, cargo damage, and component failure intervals should dominate the next two to three research cycles.

Top 3 Promising Leads This Cycle
1
i-DREAMS Cross-National Analysis
Accident Analysis & Prevention · DOI:10.1016/j.aap.2025.108307

First direct peer-reviewed evidence that intervention modality (real-time vs. gamified post-trip) must be calibrated by driver cultural context — foundational for designing a multi-market fleet coaching product that doesn't apply a one-size-fits-all feedback loop.

2
Vehicle Type, Tyre Feature & Driving Behaviour → Tire Wear (XGBoost)
Science of the Total Environment · ScienceDirect

The first peer-reviewed study to rank driving-behavior feature importance on tire wear using XGBoost on a real taxi fleet, directly informing which telemetry signals to prioritize in a maintenance-cost prediction module and filling the most critical gap in the Cost & Maintenance pillar.

3
Predicting Damage Incidents, Fines & Fuel Consumption from Truck Driver Data (Netherlands)
Transportation Research Record · SAGE Journals

Provides the first predictive model linking harsh-braking frequency per hour to both damage incidents and regulatory fines in trucks, with engine-torque-exceedance as a directly implementable CAN-bus signal for fuel-consumption prediction — a commercially deployable cost-impact building block.

Today's Strategic Position

The core scoring, alerting, and dashboard product is ready to build: Behavior Metrics & Scoring is saturated at 99%, Safety & Force Thresholds now have practitioner-grade starting values at 91%, and Service & Report Design patterns are well-documented at 83% — giving the engineering team sufficient signal to begin implementation without waiting for further research.

The single biggest commercial blocker remains the Cost & Maintenance Impact pillar at 55%: despite meaningful progress on tire wear and truck damage prediction this cycle, the product cannot yet offer fleet operators a validated quantitative "driving style → maintenance cost" calculator — the differentiator most likely to justify premium pricing.

Top priority for the next 2–3 research cycles: dose-response curves for brake wear, cargo damage claim rates, and component failure intervals, alongside per-country calibration offsets for the scoring model to support the multi-market rollout validated as necessary by the i-DREAMS findings.
