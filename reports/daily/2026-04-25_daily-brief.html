Today's Strategic Position

The core product — behavior scoring, real-time alerting, and fleet reporting — is architecturally ready for build: the four-metric composite (speeding, harsh acceleration, harsh braking, harsh cornering) is validated across 22+ independent commercial platforms and multiple peer-reviewed studies, and dashboard and intervention patterns are now well-specified.

Two blockers prevent full production confidence: BLOCKER 1 the absence of a unified, vehicle-category-specific g-force threshold table with validated false-positive rates forces conservative defaults and empirical iteration at launch; BLOCKER 2 insufficient dose-response data linking driving behavior to component wear costs caps ROI claims at fuel savings, limiting upsell into maintenance budget conversations.

The single highest-leverage next research action is sourcing or commissioning a controlled study that maps harsh-braking event frequency (per 1,000 km) to brake pad wear rate by vehicle category — it would simultaneously close the threshold calibration gap and deliver the quantified cost narrative needed to justify the product to fleet finance stakeholders.
