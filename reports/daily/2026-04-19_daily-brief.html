Today's Strategic Position
~80% Research Complete

The behavior scoring, fatigue detection, eco-driving, and real-time alerting pillars are sufficiently evidenced to begin V1 feature development immediately, with commercial and peer-reviewed sources aligned on core mechanics, thresholds, and incentive structures.

The single largest technical blocker remains the absence of peer-reviewed, vehicle-category-specific G-force threshold tables with validated false-positive rates — without these, initial thresholds must be set empirically and validated in the field, adding 4–8 weeks of confidence-building time before customers can rely on scoring accuracy.

A secondary commercial blocker is the thin dose-response evidence linking harsh-event frequency to specific maintenance cost outcomes; directional estimates are strong but insufficient for contractual SLA commitments on maintenance savings, making this the priority research target for the next cycle.
