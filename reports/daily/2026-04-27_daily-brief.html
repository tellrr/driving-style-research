Executive Summary

This cycle's dominant finding is that no single fixed deceleration threshold reliably detects harsh braking across vehicle categories and driving contexts — the Jansen et al. truck-driver study provides the first peer-reviewed empirical proof, while the dual-tier severity taxonomy (0.5 G harsh / 1.5 G severe) from data-mining research and the SafeDriver-IQ probabilistic scoring model together give product teams a concrete, implementable path forward. The four canonical behavior metrics remain fully confirmed across 35+ independent platform deployments, and scoring architecture is mature enough for production. The outstanding blocker is a unified, vehicle-category-specific threshold reference table with validated detection performance and contextual adjustment factors — its absence will extend any field-calibration cycle and elevate false-alarm risk for non-passenger-car fleets.

Today's Strategic Position
⚠ Key Blocker

The project is 85–90% of the way to a fully evidence-based service blueprint: behavioral metrics, scoring architectures, and threshold ranges are all mature enough for production implementation with high confidence across standard passenger-car and light-commercial fleets.

The single largest remaining gap is the absence of a unified, vehicle-category-specific threshold reference table with validated sensitivity, specificity, and false-positive rates, plus contextual adjustment factors for weather, road surface, and gradient — without it, any deployed system faces extended field-calibration cycles and elevated false-alarm risk, particularly for heavy-vehicle categories now confirmed to require adaptive rather than fixed thresholds.
