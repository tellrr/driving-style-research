Executive Summary
This cycle's most consequential finding is that driving style is not a fixed trait but a dynamic, context-driven process — a result with direct consequences for scoring architecture, which must model temporal evolution rather than aggregate event counts alone. Simultaneously, a randomized clinical trial confirms that automated feedback alone is insufficient to change driver behaviour; statistically significant improvement requires integrated human coaching touchpoints. The corpus now exceeds 1,165 articles across five pillars, with behaviour scoring fully saturated at 99% and overall product readiness assessed at approximately 85%.

Top 3 Promising Leads
Lead #1  ·  Scoring Architecture
Identification of Dynamic Driving Styles Based on Behavioral Primitives
Nature Scientific Reports, 2026
The first corpus study to formally prove that driving style evolves dynamically with context and intent — meaning any scoring engine that relies solely on static aggregate counts will misclassify drivers whose risk profile shifts across trip types, times of day, or load conditions, making temporal modelling a first-class architectural requirement.
Lead #2  ·  Alert & Coaching Logic
Enhancing Driving Risk Scores with Near-Miss Events
SSRN 5917802
Near-miss events deliver far more frequent and behaviourally rich signals than claims data alone, meaning a near-miss detection layer sitting above threshold-based harsh-event scoring would dramatically increase alert sensitivity and enable proactive intervention before an incident materialises.
Lead #3  ·  Coaching Workflow Design
In-Vehicle Feedback With or Without Parent Communication Training and Teenage Driving Behaviors
PubMed 42030047 — Randomized Clinical Trial
This RCT proves that automated feedback alone did not reach statistical significance for behaviour change; only the combination with structured human communication training reduced risky events (AIRR 0.68) — a direct mandate to embed human coaching touchpoints into the fleet service workflow rather than relying on alerts alone.
Today's Strategic Position
Overall Readiness: ~85%
Behaviour scoring architecture is fully validated, light-vehicle threshold ranges are well-established, and dashboard/report patterns are richly documented across 30+ commercial platforms — the product design phase is within reach. Key Blocker The absence of a vehicle-category-specific threshold calibration framework with validated false-positive and false-negative rates remains the single critical gap; deploying across mixed fleets without it will produce systematically unreliable scores for at least some vehicle categories, directly eroding fleet manager trust at the point of launch. Resolving this gap — through a controlled multi-category threshold validation study — should be the singular research priority before committing to full engineering.
