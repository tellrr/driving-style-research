Distance-Normalisation Correction · Applied to all metrics
Why distance-normalise?
Harsh event counts grow at a decreasing rate with driving distance (systematic review finding). Raw event counts unfairly penalise high-mileage drivers. Normalising per 1,000 km corrects this bias.
Sub-linear correction formula
Adjusted rate = raw events / (km^0.85). Power <1 reduces the denominator growth at high mileage, preventing over-reward of very high-mileage drivers while still correcting for distance exposure.
⚠ Road-surface quality disclaimer: accelerometer data may be affected by poor road conditions in some regions. This correction is not yet applied. Scores shown with "road conditions may affect scores" disclaimer in affected regions.
