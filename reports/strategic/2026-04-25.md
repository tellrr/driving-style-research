## 4. Overall Strategic Position

We are now in a strong position to build the core product: behavior scoring, alerting, and fleet-level reporting are architecturally defined and validated by 22+ independent commercial platforms and multiple peer-reviewed studies. The two blockers preventing full production confidence are (a) the **absence of a unified, vehicle-category-specific g-force threshold table** with validated false-positive rates — which forces us to ship with conservative defaults and iterate empirically — and (b) **insufficient quantitative dose-response data linking driving behavior to component wear costs**, which limits our ability to make credible ROI claims beyond fuel savings. The single biggest blocker is the threshold table: without it, every alert risks either being too sensitive (generating alert fatigue) or too lenient (missing genuine risk events) for specific vehicle types.

## 6. What Would You Build?

### Ready Now

1. **Composite Driver Score (0–100) with Daily/Weekly Cadence**
   The architecture is proven: weight braking, acceleration, cornering, and speeding events, normalize per distance, and produce a daily score. Azuga's published spec ([PDF](https://assets-global.website-files.com/5bcf95411e70df20404f914c/604e64130c8132f2e040ed20_AzugaFleet_Driver+Score.pdf)) provides an explicit template. CTS Telematics' dynamic weighting concept ([clevertecsystems.com](https://clevertecsystems.com/our_product/dynamic-scores-weightings/)) should be adopted to allow fleet managers to adjust emphasis (e.g., prioritize speeding over cornering for highway fleets). The systematic review finding that harsh events grow at a decreasing rate with distance ([ResearchGate](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/378846872_A_systematic_review_of_the_use_of_in-vehicle_telematics_in_monitoring_driving_behaviours)) should be incorporated as a distance-normalization correction to avoid penalizing high-mileage drivers.

2. **Time-of-Shift Risk Alerting**
   Samsara's 26% elevation in risky behavior during the first shift-tenth ([samsara.com](https://www.samsara.com/blog/samsara-data-shows-peaks-in-risky-driving-behavior)) is directly actionable. Implement heightened alert sensitivity (lower thresholds or faster trigger) during the first and last 10% of each shift, with a "settling period" indicator on the dashboard.

3. **Eco-Driving Score with Persistent Feedback Loop**
   The eco-driving decay study ([vilniustech.lt](https://journals.vilniustech.lt/index.php/Transport/article/download/12365/9898)) proves that one-off training fails within 4 months. Build a continuous eco-driving score with weekly trend graphs and push notifications. Damoov's 27% tire-wear figure ([damoov.com](https://damoov.com/eco-driving/)) and SimplyFleet's 33% MPG variance attribution ([simplyfleet.app](https://www.simplyfleet.app/blog/what-fleet-fuel-kpis-to-measure)) provide the ROI narrative for sales.

### What to Defer

- **Component-level predictive maintenance alerts** (e.g., "your brake pads will need replacement in X km based on driving style"): The qualitative causal chain is established (harsh braking → accelerated wear), but the quantitative dose-response function linking event counts to wear rates is absent. Shipping this without validated coefficients risks inaccurate predictions and credibility loss. Defer until brake-wear controlled studies are available.

- **Road-surface-quality-adjusted scoring**: We know road roughness confounds accelerometer data, but we have no quantified noise floor by IRI class. Deploying in developing markets without this correction could produce systematically biased scores. Defer the correction algorithm until IRI-telemetry calibration data is obtained; in the meantime, ship with a "road conditions may affect scores" disclaimer.

- **Nationality/region-specific baseline scoring**: The cross-cultural study ([jstage.jst.go.jp](https://www.jstage.jst.go.jp/article/easts/13/0/13_43/_article/-char/ja/)) confirms behavioral differences exist, but we lack same-fleet multi-nationality data to calibrate baselines. Defer per-nationality normalization; use fleet-relative scoring instead.

### Biggest Insight

**Eco-driving training effects decay to baseline within 4 months without continuous feedback** ([vilniustech.lt](https://journals.vilniustech.lt/index.php/Transport/article/download/12365/9898)). This is counterintuitive because most fleet operators treat driver training as a periodic event (annual or semi-annual). Combined with the incentive study showing that gamification produces *statistically significant* improvement only for average-risk drivers ([MDPI Sensors](https://www.mdpi.com/1424-8220/25/24/7433/pdf)), this means: **the product *is* the intervention** — the persistent dashboard, scores, and alerts are not merely reporting tools but the primary behavioral change mechanism. Design priority should therefore favor real-time, always-visible feedback over retrospective monthly reports. This fundamentally reframes the product from "analytics platform" to "continuous coaching system."
