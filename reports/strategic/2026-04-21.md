## 4. Overall Strategic Position

We are **ready to build the core scoring, alerting, and dashboard product** — Behavior Metrics & Scoring is saturated, Safety & Force Thresholds now have practitioner-grade starting values, and Service & Report Design patterns are well-documented. The **single biggest blocker** is the Cost & Maintenance Impact pillar (55%): despite this cycle's meaningful progress on tire wear and truck damage prediction, we still cannot offer fleet operators a validated, quantitative "driving style → maintenance cost" calculator, which is the commercial differentiator most likely to justify premium pricing. Closing this gap — particularly dose-response curves for brake wear, cargo damage, and component failure intervals — should be the top research priority for the next 2–3 cycles.

## 6. What Would You Build?

### Ready Now

1. **Four-Metric Composite Driver Score (0–100) with Configurable Thresholds**
   The industry has converged on speeding, harsh acceleration, harsh braking, and harsh cornering as the canonical inputs. The decision-adjusted framework ([Decision-adjusted driver risk predictive models](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.aap.2021.106088)) proves thresholds must be configurable per use-case. Geotab's vehicle-class guidance ([Geotab ABC](https://www.geotab.com/blog/acceleration-braking-cornering/)) provides defensible starting values. The USIC weighted-score model ([USIC](https://www.axisgroup.com/case-studies-internal/usic-identifying-high-risk-fleet-drivers-and-reducing-safety-claims)) and LexisNexis 79% lift ([LexisNexis](https://risk.lexisnexis.com/about-us/press-room/press-release/20210415-telematics-scoring-model)) validate the approach. Ship it with a vehicle-class selector (passenger car / SUV / LCV / rigid truck / articulated truck) and per-fleet threshold tuning.

2. **Real-Time Harsh-Event Alerting with Score-Trend Early Warning**
   The evidence for real-time in-cab alerts reducing risky behavior is robust (i-DREAMS [cross-national study](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.aap.2025.108307); California work-zone study; OBMS FOT). The Umbrex finding that **score trends lead claims by months** ([Umbrex](https://umbrex.com/resources/industry-analyses/how-to-analyze-an-auto-insurance-company/driver-monitoring-scoring-model-validation/)) justifies building a fleet-manager early-warning alert when any driver's rolling score deteriorates beyond a configurable threshold. Pair with the Geotab coaching checklist workflow ([Geotab coaching](https://www.geotab.com/CMS-GeneralFiles-production/NA/Blog/2024/Coaching_Checklist/Field-Services-Driver-Coaching-checklist-1495544571-Geotab-web-EN-NA-Oct24-Final-AODA.pdf)).

3. **Fuel-Efficiency Coaching Module with RAG Scorecard**
   Eco-driving evidence is overwhelming: 10–15% per-vehicle fuel savings from real-time coaching ([Edge Telematics](https://edgetelematics.com/solutions/advanced-eco-drive/)), 4–6% from RAG scorecards in UK HGVs ([FuelMarble](https://fuelmarble.com/blog/fleet-telematics-fuel-savings-uk-hgv)), with harsh acceleration, over-revving, and speed-limit non-adherence as top contributors ([freight drivers' behavior study](https://link.springer.com/content/pdf/10.1007/s12351-023-00798-2.pdf)). Build using CAN-bus RPM/torque where available, GPS speed everywhere, and generate per-driver weekly RAG reports.

### What to Defer

- **Predictive Maintenance Calculator** (brake pad / tire replacement scheduling linked to driving style): Despite this cycle's tire-wear XGBoost study and the Netherlands damage-prediction model, we lack the dose-response precision required to promise fleet operators specific maintenance-interval adjustments. Premature deployment risks credibility. **Wait for 2–3 more dose-response studies or partner with a fleet to collect proprietary data.**
- **Cargo Damage Risk Scoring**: Zero peer-reviewed evidence linking specific telemetry event patterns to cargo damage claim rates. Do not build until at least one validated model exists.
- **Country-Specific Scoring Normalization**: i-DREAMS proves cultural differences matter, but no numeric calibration offsets exist. Defer auto-adjustment; instead, allow manual fleet-level threshold tuning as a stopgap.

### Biggest Insight

**Intervention modality effectiveness is culturally mediated, not universal.** The i-DREAMS cross-national study ([i-DREAMS](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.aap.2025.108307)) found that real-time alerts reduced kinematic risk for UK drivers but not Belgian drivers, while gamified post-trip feedback worked for Belgian drivers but not UK drivers. This is strategically critical: **a single feedback UX will not work across all markets.** Our product must support multiple intervention pathways (real-time audio/visual alert, post-trip gamified report, peer leaderboard, manager coaching session) and allow fleet operators or regional deployments to select the modality mix. This finding should directly shape the architecture of the coaching and feedback subsystems.
