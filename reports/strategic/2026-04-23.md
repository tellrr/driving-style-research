## 2. Key Gaps

| Pillar | Most Critical Missing Piece |
|--------|---------------------------|
| **Behavior Metrics & Scoring** | **Cold-start ramp-curve calibration.** We still lack empirical data on how many trips/km are required before a composite score stabilizes for a new driver-vehicle pair. Without this, initial scores will be unreliable and erode trust. The short-trajectory study (Springer, prior cycle) confirms classification is possible from brief segments, but does not specify convergence dynamics of a running score. |
| **Safety & Force Thresholds** | **A complete vehicle-class-stratified threshold table with sensitivity/specificity/FPR.** We now have cars (8 m/s² braking), trucks (7 m/s² braking), and a cornering reference (5.5 m/s²), but no validated thresholds for LCVs, SUVs, buses (beyond one lateral-acceleration study), or articulated trucks. No study provides false-positive rates for any threshold. |
| **Cost & Maintenance Impact** | **Peer-reviewed dose-response curves.** Every source in the corpus states that harsh events increase wear and cost; none quantifies the function (e.g., "each additional harsh-braking event per 1,000 km reduces brake-pad life by X%"). Without this, we cannot build a credible predictive-maintenance or cost-savings module. |
| **Driver & Route Context** | **Road-surface-quality telemetry signatures and per-region threshold adjustment factors.** The context-normalized scoring patent (US20220003556A1) formalizes the concept, but no dataset or model maps road roughness to accelerometer noise floors in a way that would allow automatic threshold adjustment. |
| **Service & Report Design** | **Feedback-decay mitigation and optimal alert cadence.** The NTUA long-term feedback study shows behavioral improvements decay; the Samsara report shows positive reinforcement beats penalties. But no study provides an evidence-based alert-frequency schedule or gamification decay-prevention protocol. |

## 4. Overall Strategic Position

We have reached near-saturation on *what to measure* (behavior metrics, scoring architecture, cross-cultural context) and have achieved a working — though incomplete — set of G-force thresholds differentiated by vehicle class. **We are approximately 80% ready to move into product design for core scoring and alerting modules.** The single biggest blocker is the **absence of dose-response cost models**: without quantified links between event frequency and maintenance/damage costs, we cannot build the ROI calculators and predictive-maintenance modules that differentiate a premium offering from commodity telematics. Secondary blockers are the lack of a complete vehicle-class threshold table and the absence of evidence-based alert-cadence optimization.

## 6. What Would You Build?

### Ready Now

1. **Composite Driver Score (0–100) with Real-Time Event Tagging.** The four-metric composite (speeding, harsh acceleration, harsh braking, harsh cornering) is universally validated across 20+ commercial platforms and academic studies. Use vehicle-class-differentiated thresholds (8 m/s² braking for cars, 7 m/s² for trucks, 5.5 m/s² cornering as baseline) from the HBI study and Digital Matter documentation. Implement the Bayesian SCB approach (~7 s latency) for near-real-time in-cab feedback, falling back to trip-level aggregation using CTHMM for post-trip scoring. Include road-quality context normalization per US20220003556A1 to suppress false positives on rough roads.

2. **Weekly Driver Scorecard with Trend Visualization & Positive Reinforcement.** Directly supported by Trackstar, Geotab, FlowSense, and Netradyne guides. The Samsara distracted-driving finding that **positive reinforcement outperforms penalties** should govern the UX: highlight improvements, badge safe streaks, rank drivers against their own history (not only against peers). The NTUA feedback-decay study mandates that scorecards must be **continuous and adaptive**, not one-shot — build in escalating engagement triggers when scores plateau or regress.

3. **Eco-Driving Fuel Efficiency Module.** Fuel = 30% of fleet cost (heavyvehicleinspection.com); even 10% improvement yields substantial ROI. Link harsh-acceleration and harsh-braking event counts to per-trip fuel consumption estimates. Use Webfleet's nine-behavior framework and Frotcom's aggregation logic. This module can launch with directional cost estimates while we await dose-response precision.

### What to Defer

- **Predictive Maintenance Cost Calculator.** The causal direction (harsh events → wear) is established qualitatively by multiple sources (yanitiger.com, OxMaint, Transtrack, Frotcom), but **no peer-reviewed dose-response function exists** to convert event counts into component-failure intervals or dollar costs with confidence intervals. Building this now would require fabricating assumptions that could erode credibility. **Defer until dose-response curves are sourced or an internal study is commissioned.**

- **Cargo Damage Risk Score.** The impact-recorder article (techwibe.com) and the miniature-truck crash study (MDPI) provide only directional evidence. No model links specific G-force event profiles to cargo damage claim rates. **Defer until claim-rate correlation data is available.**

- **Per-Region Threshold Auto-Calibration.** The concept is formalized (US20220003556A1) and the cross-cultural studies confirm the need, but no dataset maps road-surface roughness to accelerometer noise floors. **Defer until road-quality telemetry signature research is available.**

### Biggest Insight

**Feedback effects on driver behavior decay over time** (NTUA long-term study) — this is counterintuitive to the "set it and forget it" assumption embedded in most fleet telematics platforms. Combined with the Samsara finding that positive reinforcement outperforms penalties, this implies that **the engagement system is not an add-on but the core product differentiator**. A scoring system that merely generates weekly reports will see diminishing returns within months. Continuous adaptive engagement — escalating challenges, streak-based rewards, peer competitions with opt-in visibility, and manager-triggered coaching touchpoints — is not a gamification layer; it is a **retention and efficacy requirement** backed by longitudinal evidence.
