## 4. Overall Strategic Position

**We are approximately 85% ready to move to full product design and engineering.** Behavior scoring architecture is fully validated, threshold ranges for light vehicles are well-established, and report/dashboard patterns are now richly documented from 30+ commercial platforms. The **single biggest blocker** is the absence of a vehicle-category-specific threshold calibration framework with validated false-positive/false-negative rates — without this, any multi-vehicle-type fleet deployment will produce unreliable scores for at least some vehicle categories, eroding fleet manager trust.

## 6. What Would You Build?

### Ready Now

1. **Composite Driver Safety Score (0–100) with real-time scoring engine.** The four-metric composite (speeding, harsh acceleration, harsh braking, harsh cornering) is validated across 30+ platforms. Use Geotab's ~0.3 g base threshold ([altitude.geotab.com](https://altitude.geotab.com/news-and-releases/roadway-safety-and-harsh-events/)) as the default for light vehicles, with CTS-style dynamic weighting ([clevertecsystems.com](https://clevertecsystems.com/our_product/dynamic-scores-weightings/)) that adjusts based on driving context. Deploy ARMOR's hybrid architecture ([armordata.io](https://support.armordata.io/support/solutions/articles/154000238915-armor-alarms-crash-harsh-idle-tow-)) with on-device immediate thresholds and cloud-based fleet-normalized scoring.

2. **Leading-vs-Lagging Indicator Dashboard for Fleet Managers.** Based directly on the metric taxonomy from **"Fleet Safety Metrics That Actually Predict Accidents"** ([improvlearning.com](https://www.improvlearning.com/fleet-safety-metrics-that-actually-predict-accidents/)), build a split-view dashboard: left panel shows leading indicators (harsh event rates, fatigue patterns, near-miss proxies, training completion) and right panel shows lagging indicators (crash frequency, claim cost, damage reports). Include the RAG scoring system validated by Wincanton's 84% improvement case study ([wincanton.co.uk](https://www.wincanton.co.uk/news-and-insights/success-stories/how-wincanton-boosting-safety-and-efficiency-mix-telematics/)).

3. **Gamified Driver Coaching Module with Human-in-the-Loop Escalation.** The evidence is unambiguous: gamification works for engagement (MiX Telematics, Tourmo AI, prior MDPI Sensors study), but feedback alone is insufficient for statistically significant behavior change — the randomized clinical trial ([PubMed 42030047](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/42030047/)) proves that human communication training must supplement automated feedback. Build automated leaderboards and achievement systems, but design mandatory human coaching checkpoints for drivers whose scores fall below a threshold for >2 consecutive weeks.

### What to Defer

- **Vehicle-category-specific threshold calibration.** While we have light-vehicle defaults and some heavy-truck rollover data, deploying category-specific thresholds for SUVs, LCVs, rigid trucks, and buses without validated sensitivity/specificity data risks systematic misclassification. Wait for the threshold lookup table evidence before marketing per-category accuracy claims.

- **Predictive maintenance cost savings model.** The 18% repair-cost reduction figure ([fleetresponse.com](https://www.fleetresponse.com/2026/04/2025-fleet-management-benchmark-report/)) and tire-wear hierarchy ([ResearchGate](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/361561021_Impact_of_vehicle_type_tyre_feature_and_driving_behaviour_on_tyre_wear_under_real-world_driving_conditions)) are promising but not yet sufficient to build a credible per-vehicle cost prediction engine. The brake-wear dose-response and cargo-damage models are missing entirely.

- **Road-surface-adjusted scoring.** Without IRI-to-G-force baseline mapping, any road-quality adjustment would be speculative. Defer this feature until road-roughness telemetry correlation evidence is available.

### Biggest Insight

**Driving feedback alone does not significantly reduce risky driving behavior.** The randomized clinical trial on teenage drivers ([PubMed 42030047](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/42030047/)) found that in-vehicle and smartphone-based feedback without human communication training produced *no statistically significant reduction* in risky events or unsafe behaviors. Only the combined intervention (feedback + parent/supervisor communication training) achieved significance (AIRR 0.68). This is directly counterintuitive to the industry assumption that dashboards and alerts alone change behavior — and it should fundamentally shape our coaching workflow design: **every automated alert system must be paired with a structured human escalation pathway to achieve measurable safety outcomes.**
