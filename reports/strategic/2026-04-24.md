## 4. Overall Strategic Position

**We have sufficient evidence to build and ship a production-grade driver behavior scoring and alerting platform today.** The core scoring architecture (four-metric composite, 0–100 scale, G-force-based event detection) is validated by 15+ independent commercial implementations and multiple academic studies; context-adaptive thresholds are supported by peer-reviewed naturalistic driving data; and ROI evidence (65% collision-cost reduction, 90% overspeeding reduction, 40% high-risk score improvement in 3 months) is strong enough for go-to-market positioning. **The single biggest blocker is the absence of quantitative dose-response models linking harsh-event frequency/severity to specific maintenance cost outcomes** — without these, the "maintenance savings" value proposition remains directional rather than calculable, limiting our ability to build credible cost-savings dashboards and forcing reliance on fleet operators' own historical data for calibration.

## 6. What Would You Build?

### Ready Now

1. **Core Driver Behavior Scoring Engine (0–100 composite score)**
   Build the four-metric composite scorer (speeding, harsh acceleration, harsh braking, harsh cornering) normalized per km or per hour, using the architecture validated across Zubie, Geotab, Cartrack, SimplyFleet, and the Scotto system (<10% error, [research.bau.edu.tr](https://research.bau.edu.tr/en/publications/scotto-real-time-driver-behavior-scoring-using-in-vehicle-data-2/)). Implement ML-based event classification rather than pure rule-based thresholds, targeting the ~30% FPR reduction demonstrated by [ResearchGate/Toheeb](https://www.researchgate.net/profile/Oladeji-Toheeb/publication/393872316_Using_Telematics_Data_and_Machine_Learning_to_Refine_Risk_Profiles/links/687e71511a77b36b5b046429/Using-Telematics-Data-and-Machine-Learning-to-Refine-Risk-Profiles.pdf). Use Geotab's enhanced safety rules approach ([support.geotab.com](https://support.geotab.com/product-updates/enchanced-harsh-safety-rules)) as the design template: rules off by default, severity-tiered, false-alarm-minimized.

2. **Context-Adaptive Threshold Engine with Vehicle-Class Differentiation**
   Deploy initial thresholds using the evidence base we have: harsh braking at **8 m/s² for cars, 7 m/s² for trucks** ([1library.net](https://1library.net/article/harsh-braking-incidents-near-miss-accidents-and-hbis.zx0lvlwz)), cornering at **5.5 m/s²** baseline (Digital Matter, prior cycle), and conservative HGV lateral thresholds informed by the rollover study showing actual lateral G exceeds design values ([doi.org/10.3141/1851-03](https://doi.org/10.3141/1851-03)). Implement the adaptive-threshold approach validated by [ScienceDirect/S0001457521000828](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S0001457521000828) and the truck-context study ([ResearchGate/377665270](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/377665270_Harsh_Braking_by_Truck_Drivers_A_Comparison_of_Thresholds_and_Driving_Contexts_Using_Naturalistic_Driving_Data)), adjusting thresholds based on road type (urban vs. highway) and vehicle category.

3. **Spatial Harsh-Event Heat Map with Location-Based Alerting**
   The spatial clustering evidence ([PubMed 34015603](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/34015603/)), Coaster's route-tied braking data ([stopclusters.com](https://stopclusters.com/)), and Nexar's behavioral maps ([medium.com/mamane](https://mamane.medium.com/behavioral-maps-how-real-time-driving-patterns-are-uncovering-the-actual-rules-of-the-road-bff1b6bd1c26)) all converge: build a fleet-wide heat map of harsh events overlaid on road segments, with proactive alerts for drivers approaching known high-risk zones.

### What to Defer

- **Maintenance cost-savings calculator/dashboard**: The causal direction is well-established but without dose-response curves, any calculator would rely on unvalidated assumptions. Defer until we have peer-reviewed quantitative models or accumulate enough internal fleet data to derive our own curves. Display directional indicators ("high brake stress — schedule inspection sooner") rather than false-precision dollar estimates.
- **Cargo-damage prediction alerts**: The IOP Science study ([iopscience.iop.org/1247/1/012038](https://iopscience.iop.org/article/10.1088/1757-899X/1247/1/012038)) provides initial experimental data, but we lack validated thresholds across cargo types and securing methods. Build data collection infrastructure now; defer alerting until thresholds are calibrated.
- **Road-surface-quality auto-classification**: No validated algorithm exists in our corpus for distinguishing rough-road G-spikes from driver-caused events using accelerometer data alone. This risks systemic false positives in markets with poor infrastructure. Defer automatic classification; for now, allow manual route-quality tagging.

### Biggest Insight

**Actual lateral accelerations on loaded tractor-trailers consistently exceed the values predicted by roadway geometric design, approaching rollover thresholds even at legal speeds** ([doi.org/10.3141/1851-03](https://doi.org/10.3141/1851-03)). This is directly counterintuitive — it means that a truck driving at the posted speed limit on a designed curve can still be at rollover risk, and that cornering alerts for HGVs must be calibrated more conservatively than road-design standards would suggest. This single finding should fundamentally shape our HGV cornering-alert threshold design: we cannot simply use speed-limit compliance as a proxy for safe cornering.

## 7. Report & Graph Suggestions

### 1. Driver Safety Composite Score — Gauge + Trend Line

- **Name & type**: Driver Safety Score Dashboard — gauge (current score) + line chart (weekly trend)
- **Key dimensions**: Score (0–100) on gauge; time (weeks) on x-axis, score on y-axis; color zones (green/amber/red). Per-driver breakdown with fleet-average overlay.
- **Research basis**: The 0–100 scoring framework is validated by 15+ platforms including Scotto (<10% error, [research.bau.edu.tr](https://research.bau.edu.tr/en/publications/scotto-real-time-driver-behavior-scoring-using-in-vehicle-data-2/)) and the SimplyFleet KPI framework ([simplyfleet.app](https://www.simplyfleet.app/blog/what-fleet-fuel-kpis-to-measure)). Insurance Journal confirms highest-risk scores improve 40% within 3 months ([insurancejournal.com](https://www.insurancejournal.com/blogs/risk-insurance-education-alliance/2026/01/26/855308.htm)), making weekly trending essential to track improvement.
- **Audience**: Fleet manager (primary), operations director (aggregate fleet view)

### 2. Harsh Event Frequency Heat Map

- **Name & type**: Harsh Event Geographic Heat Map — spatial heat map overlaid on road network
- **Key dimensions**: GPS coordinates (lat/long), event type (braking/acceleration/cornering), severity tier, time-of-day filter. Cluster density per road segment.
- **Research basis**: Spatial clustering of harsh events confirmed by [PubMed 34015603](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/34015603/); Coaster® demonstrates that location-specific feedback outperforms general warnings ([stopclusters.com](https://stopclusters.com/)); Nexar behavioral maps show real-time patterns diverge from road design assumptions ([medium.com/mamane](https://mamane.medium.com/behavioral-maps-how-real-time-driving-patterns-are-uncovering-the-actual-rules-of-the-road-bff1b6bd1c26)).
- **Audience**: Fleet manager (route optimization), operations director (infrastructure investment decisions)

### 3. Vehicle-Class Threshold Compliance Table

- **Name & type**: Threshold Compliance Summary — table with RAG status indicators
- **Key dimensions**: Rows = vehicle classes (car, LCV, truck, articulated, bus); columns = event types (braking, acceleration, cornering); cells = events per 100 km with RAG status against class-specific thresholds; filter by time period and driver.
- **Research basis**: Vehicle-class-differentiated thresholds (8 m/s² cars, 7 m/s² trucks) from [1library.net](https://1library.net/article/harsh-braking-incidents-near-miss-accidents-and-hbis.zx0lvlwz); context-dependent thresholds validated by [ResearchGate/377665270](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/377665270_Harsh_Braking_by_Truck_Drivers_A_Comparison_of_Thresholds_and_Driving_Contexts_Using_Naturalistic_Driving_Data); rollover proximity for HGVs from [doi.org/10.3141/1851-03](https://doi.org/10.3141/1851-03).
- **Audience**: Fleet manager (mixed-fleet operations), safety compliance officer
