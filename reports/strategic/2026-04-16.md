| **Behavior Metrics & Scoring** | 95% | **Near-complete. This cycle massively reinforced scorecard design, smartphone-based detection, and sensor fusion pipelines.** The evidence base now spans the full stack from raw sensor ingestion through composite scoring to driver-facing feedback. Scorecard best-practice is now thoroughly documented across six independent industry sources (GoMotive, FleetResponse, SafetyCulture, GPS Insight, Fleet Pins, US Chamber), converging on the same design principles: normalize for exposure, weight high-risk events, pair with coaching, and contextualize by route/vehicle/role. Data quality directly impacts risk rankings: Geotab's white paper shows lower sampling rates cause misclassification of high-risk drivers as low-risk ([Putting fleet data quality to test](https://www.geotab.com/white-paper/fleet-data-quality/)). ## 6. What Would You Build?

### Ready Now

1. **Context-aware driver risk scorecard with exposure normalization.** The evidence is overwhelming: combine harsh braking, harsh acceleration, harsh cornering, and speeding events weighted by road type and time of day ([GPS-Observed Driving Behavior Exposure Metrics](https://trid.trb.org/view/1289262)), normalize by mileage/trip duration, apply CRITIC or entropy-AHP weights ([Driving Behavior Risk Measurement and Cluster Analysis](https://www.mdpi.com/2076-3417/13/9/5675/pdf?version=1683257570); [Analysis of taxi driving behavior](https://doi.org/10.1109/ivs.2019.8813887)), and output a 0–100 score with transparent sub-component breakdown. Pair with personal-best or personal-average nudges depending on driver tier ([Nudging Drivers to Safety](https://doi.org/10.1287/mnsc.2021.4063)). Implement a data-quality gate: flag drivers below minimum trip/distance thresholds and communicate score confidence ([Geotab data quality](https://www.geotab.com/white-paper/fleet-data-quality/)).

2. **Configurable harsh-event detection engine with vehicle-class presets.** Use Verizon Connect thresholds as starting defaults for light vehicles (hard braking >0.265 g ≥1 s, hard cornering >0.4 g ≥2 s) and HPV empirical bounds for heavy vehicles (±0.27–0.42 g) ([Verizon Connect](https://www.verizonconnect.com/resources/article/harsh-braking-acceleration-why-monitor/); [Assessing safety critical driving patterns HPV](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/34735888/)). Apply minimum speed gates to filter low-speed false positives ([Samsara](https://kb.samsara.com/hc/en-us/articles/5321169919501-Harsh-Event-Detection)), cross-reference with RPM where CAN is available ([Piritiz](https://piritiz.com/gps-detects-speeding-harsh-driving-2/)), and allow per-fleet override. Log all sub-threshold events for offline threshold tuning.

3. **Fuel-efficiency and eco-driving score.** ISAAC-style metrics (top-gear cruising fraction, steady-speed ratio, idle time) are validated against hard-braking, hard-turning, and collision outcomes ([Effect of eco-driving on CMV collision risk](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/38858043/)). The ~5% inter-style fuel gap and 10–30% aggressive-vs-calm range are publishable benchmarks ([Benchmarking driver acceleration impact](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.trd.2022.103282); [Impact of Driving Style on Emissions](https://doi.org/10.2139/ssrn.4012807)). This score can be delivered today as a standalone eco-driving report or fused into the composite driver score.

### What to Defer

- **Total cost-of-bad-driving calculator** (brake wear, tire wear, suspension, cargo damage). The pillar sits at 28% with no peer-reviewed quantification of event-to-cost relationships. Shipping this now would require assumptions that could undermine credibility. Wait for dedicated studies or commission internal fleet data analysis.
- **Cultural/nationality-based driver profiling.** No evidence base exists in the corpus; building this prematurely risks stereotyping without validation.
- **Advanced UX optimization** (alert modality, frequency, dashboard layout). Scorecard content is well-defined, but we lack controlled UX studies. Ship a v1 dashboard informed by converged industry best practice, then plan internal A/B tests.

### Biggest Insight

**Vehicle mass systematically biases accelerometer-derived safety scores** ([Telematics Truths](https://orionfi.com/telematics-truths/)). A 40-ton HGV will almost never trigger the same g-force thresholds as a 2-ton van for the same driver inputs, meaning fleets with mixed vehicle classes will systematically under-score heavy vehicles and over-score light ones unless mass normalization is applied. This is not an edge case — it is a fundamental scoring fairness issue that most competing platforms do not address, and correcting for it would be a differentiating feature.

## 7. Report & Graph Suggestions

### 7.1 Driver Safety Scorecard — Single Driver View
- **Type:** Composite dashboard (gauge + bar breakdown + trend line)
- **Key dimensions:** Overall score (0–100 gauge), sub-scores for harsh braking, acceleration, cornering, speeding, and distraction (horizontal bar chart), 12-week trend line of overall score
- **Research basis:** Converged scorecard best-practice from [GoMotive](https://gomotive.com/blog/driver-scorecard-guide/), [SafetyCulture](https://safetyculture.com/topics/fleet-safety/driver-scorecard), and [GPS Insight](https://www.gpsinsight.com/blog/driver-scorecards-your-solution-to-better-fleet-training/); CRITIC-weighted sub-component transparency from [Driving Behavior Risk Measurement and Cluster Analysis](https://www.mdpi.com/2076-3417/13/9/5675/pdf?version=1683257570)
- **Audience:** Fleet manager, driver (self-service portal)

### 7.2 Fleet Risk Distribution — Driver Tier Histogram
- **Type:** Stacked bar chart or histogram
- **Key dimensions:** X-axis = risk tier (e.g., Conservative / Safe / Moderate / Aggressive / Dangerous); Y-axis = number of drivers; color = vehicle category
- **Research basis:** Five-tier classification from [Driving Risk Assessment Using Near-Miss Events](https://www.mdpi.com/1099-4300/23/7/829/pdf?version=1654825417); four-tier K-means segmentation from [Driving Behavior Risk Measurement and Cluster Analysis](https://www.mdpi.com/2076-3417/13/9/5675/pdf?version=1683257570)
- **Audience:** Fleet manager, operations director

### 7.3 Harsh-Event Heat Map — Spatial and Temporal
- **Type:** Geographic heat map overlaid on road network + time-of-day matrix
- **Key dimensions:** Latitude/longitude clusters of harsh events; secondary view: hour-of-day × day-of-week heat grid; filter by event type (braking/acceleration/cornering)
- **Research basis:** GPS-Observed Exposure Metrics showing road-type × time-of-day interaction effects ([Potential GPS-Observed Driving Behavior Exposure Metrics](https://trid.trb.org/view/1289262)); roadway geometry × grade influence ([Quantifying the effect of roadway, driver, vehicle, and location characteristics](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/34455341/))

### 7.4 Eco-Driving Performance Report
