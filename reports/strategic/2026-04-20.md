## 2. Key Gaps

| Pillar | Most Critical Missing Piece |
|--------|----------------------------|
| **Behavior Metrics & Scoring** | **Cold-start calibration curve**: No published study defines the minimum number of trips or kilometers needed before a new driver's score stabilizes to a reliable level, segmented by vehicle category. Without this, platforms risk premature labeling of new drivers as risky or safe. |
| **Safety & Force Thresholds** | **Vehicle-class-specific threshold matrix**: Despite the decision-adjusted framework proving thresholds must be context-sensitive, no peer-reviewed source provides a validated G-force lookup table (sensitivity, specificity, FPR) across passenger car, SUV, LCV, rigid truck, and articulated truck categories. This is the single most critical technical gap for product design. |
| **Cost & Maintenance Impact** | **Dose-response quantification**: We have broad directional evidence (15–40% fuel penalty, 20–30% brake/tire wear reduction) but no published model that maps harsh-event *count × severity* to specific maintenance outcomes (e.g., "each additional harsh braking event per 1,000 km at severity X reduces brake pad life by Y%"). This prevents us from building a credible maintenance-cost prediction feature. |
| **Driver & Route Context** | **Road-surface and weather interaction effects**: Beyond the single Nairobi pothole study, we lack systematic data on how road roughness indices (IRI) and weather conditions shift telemetry baselines. Without this, scoring models will penalize drivers on poor roads or in rain/snow. |
| **Service & Report Design** | **Controlled UI/UX evaluation**: No A/B or controlled study compares specific dashboard visualizations (gauge vs. bar, real-time trend vs. weekly summary, league table vs. individual profile) on fleet manager decision quality or driver behavioral outcomes. The design pattern catalogue is rich but empirically unranked. |

## 4. Overall Strategic Position

We are approximately **80% ready** to move to full product design. Behavior scoring methodology is effectively saturated — industry-validated, commercially deployed, and now supported by sophisticated algorithmic options from genetic programming to federated learning. Service and report design patterns have converged strongly, and coaching intervention evidence is robust. **The single biggest blocker is the absence of a peer-reviewed, vehicle-class-specific G-force threshold matrix with validated sensitivity/specificity**: this prevents us from setting defensible default thresholds out-of-the-box and forces every deployment to rely on per-fleet calibration, which increases onboarding cost and time-to-value. The secondary blocker — absence of dose-response maintenance cost models — prevents us from building the maintenance-prediction differentiator that would set our platform apart from competitors.

## 6. What Would You Build?

### Ready Now

**1. Configurable Multi-Metric Driver Score with Decision-Adjusted Thresholds**
Build a composite 0–100 driver score blending acceleration, braking, cornering, speed, and distraction metrics. Crucially, implement the decision-adjusted framework from [Decision-adjusted driver risk predictive models](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.aap.2021.106088): allow fleet operators to choose their objective (e.g., "flag top 5% riskiest" vs. "flag all above absolute danger level") and have the system automatically optimize thresholds to maximize precision for that decision rule. The FICO and CMT commercial models ([FICO](https://www.fico.com/en/products/fico-safe-driving-score); [CMT](https://www.cmtelematics.com/drivewell-score/)) validate that telematics-only scoring (no demographics) is commercially viable and regulatorily approved. XGBoost-based feature extraction ([Evaluation and interpretation of driving risks](https://doi.org/10.1002/sam.11599)) provides the recommended ML backbone.

**2. Real-Time Coaching Alert Pipeline with Tiered Escalation**
Deploy in-cab alerts for harsh events (braking, acceleration, cornering, speeding) using the 32 ms edge-inference architecture validated by GNN-RMNet ([GNN-RMNet](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/40773479/)). Structure the coaching workflow per the OBMS FOT findings ([Performance Assessment of an OBMS](https://doi.org/10.21949/1502658)): in-cab audio/visual alert → automated self-coaching module → manager-supported session for persistent offenders. The Samsara model of 45+ factor prioritization ([Samsara Safety Report](https://www.samsara.com/resources/fleet-safety-report)) confirms that AI-prioritized event escalation outperforms flat alerting. Use the Schlumberger evidence ([Driver Monitors](https://doi.org/10.2118/86834-ms)) to set realistic KPI targets: 50% incident reduction over 2–3 years.

**3. Fleet Safety Benchmarking Dashboard with External Comparison**
Implement a dashboard following the Geotab Safety Benchmarks model ([Fleet Safety Benchmarks](https://www.geotab.com/blog/fleet-safety-benchmarks/)): allow fleet managers to compare their fleet's safety metrics against anonymized industry averages. Include the standard KPI set confirmed across multiple sources this cycle: harsh event rates per 1,000 km, speeding percentage, driver score distribution, and fuel efficiency index. Design follows the evidence that centralized multi-metric dashboards outperform siloed views ([Fleet Management Dashboard: KPIs](https://heavyvehicleinspection.com/article/fleet-management-dashboard-kpi-analytics)).

### What to Defer

- **Maintenance-cost prediction engine**: The evidence is directional only (15–40% fuel penalty, up to 30% component life extension). Without dose-response curves linking event frequency × severity to specific component replacement intervals, any maintenance prediction feature would be speculative. **Wait for brake/tire/suspension wear data before productizing.**
- **Road-surface-adjusted scoring**: We have only the Nairobi case study showing potholes trigger false harsh-braking events. Building an IRI-based correction layer requires systematic road-surface telemetry data across multiple geographies. **Defer until road-quality datasets are sourced.**
- **Country-specific scoring calibration offsets**: Cross-cultural evidence exists (Israel, Queensland, South Africa) but is insufficient to generate per-country calibration tables. **Defer formal offsets; use the configurable threshold framework as a stopgap.**

### Biggest Insight

**There is no single "correct" harsh-event threshold.** The decision-adjusted framework ([Decision-adjusted driver risk predictive models](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.aap.2021.106088)) formally proves that the optimal acceleration/deceleration threshold shifts by up to 31.8% depending on the fleet operator's decision objective (flag top 1% vs. top 10%, insurance pricing vs. safety coaching). This demolishes the common industry practice of hardcoded, one-size-fits-all thresholds and means our platform **must** expose a configurable "decision objective" parameter per customer rather than shipping fixed defaults. This single design choice — making thresholds adaptive to the business question, not just the sensor data — is the strongest differentiator available in the current evidence base.
